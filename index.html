Nationality Council News Origin

The archive titled, “Nationality Council News vol. 21” is about the fiftieth anniversary of the completion of the Nationality Rooms Program in November of 1976. It can be found as a digitized historical newsletter, preserved in the University of Pittsburgh’s Digital Collection, among 102 additional newsletters regarding the Nationality Council. The archive serves as a primary source for becoming more informed of the organization’s activities and priorities, during the time of publication. It is a newsletter published by the Office of Cultural and Educational Exchange and from Nationality Council News. From the University Secretary and Nationality Council Chairman, John Brosky, included is a list of recent events held by the Committees and a schedule of when television specials and articles relating to the anniversary are to be released.

21st Volume of the Nationality Council News

50th Aniversary of the Nationality Council

There are recent updates and announcements included from each Nationality Room Committee intended to share with members of the National Council. The 21st volume of the National Council Newsletters was originally printed by the National Council under the director of Pitt’s Nationality Rooms and Exchange program, Maxine Bruhns, who recently passed in 2020 after serving for over 50 years, according to The Pitt News and is now digitalized and preserved by the University (O’Donnell, 2020). Recent and upcoming events from each committee are mentioned as well as a new book for the nationality rooms including photos of each room alongside a new map which is advertised and available for sale. Following are the TV specials to commemorate the fiftieth anniversary listed with a full schedule accompanying recent meetings that were held and are listed with notes and explanations. Last, in the newsletter is a full calendar of upcoming events and meetings being held by committees of the nationality rooms. This archive is very similar to others in the collection, the main difference being that this newsletter is mainly focused on the fiftieth anniversary and events celebrating that. It functions as a historical record, so that researchers and students can read and study it to establish what issues and decisions were relevant at the time. It can also offer context for trends, social aspects, and history in organizational terms. In relation to other information in the archive, it fits chronologically into the full publications of the National Council News, so the ideas, policies, and memberships can be seen as evolving over time.
